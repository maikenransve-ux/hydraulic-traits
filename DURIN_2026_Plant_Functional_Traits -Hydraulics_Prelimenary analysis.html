Testing for normality

The dependent variable should be approximately normally distributed within each group. While ANOVA is relatively robust to moderate violations of normality, especially with larger sample sizes, extreme departures can affect validity.

-Shapiro-Wilk test reveals non-normality for the linear model, but since we have a large data set n>50, the test is too sensitive and results might be a false negative, leading to rejection of normal data, when it is in reality fine to use.

-Both ANOVA and mixed models are robust to large datasets when it comes to deviating slightly from normality

Account for random effects and data clustering with lmer

→ Fixed effects with interactions: trait ~  NIN*age class, effect of NiN type on trait also depends on effect of age class, and possibly plant sampling groups

-Plant ID (plant nr) is a grouping factor that should be used as random effects to account for non-independence. To account for “within-individual” grouping. These are nested groups with lower level units contained within higher levels

→ often use mixed-effects models as factors with few levels are fixed (NiN, age class) and factors with many levels are random (Plant 1-7 grouping)

5 leaf bulks ⊂ 5 samples ⊂ Plant 1-7 ⊂ Age class × NiN type


-Linear mixed effects models, lme4 package, diff model, but same anova, summary etc. 1I plantID, not for height due to low replicate number

-When you move to lme4 for mixed-effects models, you have two types of normality to check:

  • Residual normality: Just like ANOVA, the overall errors of the model should look fairly normal on a Q-Q plot.

  • Random effects normality: The random intercepts or slopes you estimate for your groups (like subjects or schools) should also follow a normal distribution.

Interpret emmeans: emmean column means the adjusted average for each trait in the same units

t-ratio tells you how many Standard Errors (SE) your group ratio is away from the null hypothesis, Significant: A t-ratio greater than 2 or less than -2 is generally statistically significant (assuming a standard 5% error rate).

Not Significant: A t-ratio close to 0 (between -2 and 2) means the groups are too similar to be sure of any real difference

Data transformation?

-data often natural log transformed and scaled to fairly compare across traits, we can statistically contrast the different traits side-by-side: makes skewed distributions more normal by pulling extreme outliers inward; allows interpretation in multiplicative steps (1x increase → 10x increase etc.); prevents heteroscedasticity (data artificially widening as mean increases); when comparing multiple traits, scaling makes sure a change of one unit in one trait means the same across all variables

-Never use p-values to judge the success of a transformation! We use diagnostic plots to make that assessment. What we hope is that we can transform the response variable so that it conforms, at least approximately, to the assumptions of the statistical model

-transform traits individually and do back-transformation

-Plot for height is slightly heavy tailed and deviates slightly from the qqline, indicating data contains a few more extreme values than would be expected from a normal distribution. Scale plot also shows variance increases with fitted values —> try transformation

Multivariate analysis and PCA

-Because traits are not independent, we should investigate interactions between them

-The primary goal of MANOVA is to determine whether the means of the dependent variables differ significantly across groups while considering the interrelationships between the variables

-MANOVA tests whether groups differ in the multivariate combination of traits rather than running many separate ANOVAs, it tests if overall trait syndrome differs among age classes, NiN types and the interaction

-Use PCA to reduce dimensionality and identify major axes of trait covariation across NiN types and age classes (remember Diaz et al. 2016 method)

-Because leaf traits often covary (e.g., LES), check for high multicollinearity. Highly correlated variables ((r > 0.85) or (0.90)) may skew PCA loadings toward specific traits –> i.e remove wet mass, dry mass
